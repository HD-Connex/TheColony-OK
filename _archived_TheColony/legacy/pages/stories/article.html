The Lobbyist Network Funding Oklahoma's Silence

Follow the money. Our three-month investigation traces dark money from out-of-state PACs straight to the legislators who killed transparency legislation last session — and to the consulting firms who quietly bridge the gap.

SARAH MITCHELL INVESTIGATIONS · MAY 28 2026
6 MIN READ UPDATED 11:42 CT

On a Tuesday afternoon in March, three lobbyists walked out of a closed-door meeting in the basement of the Oklahoma State Capitol. Within seventy-two hours, House Bill 1247 — a bipartisan transparency measure that had passed committee unanimously — was dead.

Eight weeks of public records requests, donor disclosures, and conversations with seven current and former staffers tell the story of how it happened. It is not a story about ideology. It is a story about a network of consulting firms, out-of-state political action committees, and a small group of legislators whose campaigns happen to be funded by the same handful of donors.

This is the first of three reports.

The Coalition That Wasn't

The Oklahoma Free Markets Coalition — the group whose public position killed HB 1247 — has no employees, no Oklahoma office, and as of this filing, no website. Its registered agent is a Delaware mail-forwarding service. Its 2025 federal filings list a single contact: a Beltway consulting firm called Meridian Public Strategies.

"There is a category of organization in this state that exists only to file testimony. They show up the morning of a vote, they leave the same afternoon, and they take their money with them." — former House staffer, granted anonymity to discuss internal deliberations

Three other "coalitions" with similar profiles testified against HB 1247 between February and April. All four share the same registered agent. All four wired their lobbying disbursements through Meridian. None of them have any verifiable Oklahoma membership.

What the Filings Show

Records obtained from the Oklahoma Ethics Commission show $284,000 in disbursements from Meridian-linked entities in the first quarter of 2026 alone — roughly four times the amount any in-state advocacy group spent on the same legislation. Of that, $187,000 flowed to four lobbyists who filed testimony against HB 1247.
